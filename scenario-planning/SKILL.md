# 🔮 Scenario Planning (시나리오 플래닝)

## Shell/Schwartz Method — Strategic Foresight Through Multiple Plausible Futures

---

## Why Prediction Fails and Scenario Planning Works (왜 예측은 실패하고 시나리오 플래닝은 성공하는가)

**The fundamental problem**: Human beings are terrible at predicting the future. We anchor on current trends, ignore discontinuities, and confuse confidence with accuracy. Every economic forecast, technology prediction, and strategic plan that assumes a single future is fragile.

**The Shell Oil case study**: In the early 1970s, Royal Dutch Shell was the weakest of the "Seven Sisters" oil companies. Pierre Wack and his team in Shell's Group Planning division developed scenario planning as an alternative to traditional forecasting. While competitors used single-point oil price forecasts, Shell built multiple scenarios — including one where oil prices would spike dramatically.

When OPEC's 1973 embargo hit, Shell was the only major oil company that had already rehearsed this future mentally. They responded faster, repositioned assets earlier, and within two decades rose from last to first or second in profitability among the majors. They repeated this advantage during the 1979 oil crisis.

**The key insight**: Scenario planning does not predict the future. It **prepares the mind** to recognize and respond to multiple futures. Peter Schwartz (who later headed Shell's scenario planning group, succeeding Wack) called this "the gentle art of re-perceiving" — breaking mental models so that when change arrives, it feels like "memories of the future" rather than shock.

**When to use scenario planning (시나리오 플래닝 적용 시점)**:
- Strategic decisions with long time horizons (3-10+ years)
- High uncertainty environments where key variables are unknowable
- Investment decisions that are expensive or impossible to reverse
- When the organization is stuck in groupthink or anchored on one view
- When "what if?" questions keep leadership awake at night

## The Scenario Planning Process (시나리오 플래닝 프로세스) — 8 Steps

### Step 1: Identify the Focal Question (핵심 질문 정의)

The focal question frames everything. It must be:
- **Specific** enough to guide research and analysis
- **Strategic** enough to matter for resource allocation
- **Time-bounded** with a clear horizon (typically 5-10 years)

**Good focal questions**:
- "What will the competitive landscape for industrial services in Korea look like in 2030?"
- "How will AI and automation reshape our workforce needs over the next 5 years?"
- "What market position should we target given energy transition uncertainties?"

**Bad focal questions**:
- "What will the future look like?" (too vague)
- "Will our revenue grow next quarter?" (too short-term, use forecasting instead)

### Step 2: List Driving Forces (동인 도출)

Brainstorm all forces that could shape the answer to your focal question. Use PESTEL as a starting framework but go deeper:

| Category | Examples (산업서비스 맥락) |
|----------|--------------------------|
| **P**olitical (정치) | Environmental regulations, government industrial policy, trade tensions |
| **E**conomic (경제) | Oil prices, industrial capex cycles, interest rates, currency |
| **S**ocial (사회) | Labor shortages, aging workforce, safety culture evolution |
| **T**echnological (기술) | AI/robotics in industrial maintenance, IoT sensors, remote inspection |
| **E**nvironmental (환경) | Climate regulations, circular economy mandates, ESG requirements |
| **L**egal (법률) | Labor law changes, liability frameworks, industry certification |

Also consider:
- **Industry-specific forces**: Customer consolidation, outsourcing trends, new entrants
- **Wild cards (와일드카드)**: Pandemic-like disruptions, major industrial accidents, breakthrough technologies

**Aim for 15-30 driving forces** in the initial brainstorm. Quality comes from quantity at this stage.

### Step 3: Rank by Uncertainty and Impact (불확실성 × 영향력 평가)

Plot each driving force on a 2x2 grid:

```
         High Impact (영향력 높음)
              │
    Important │  Critical
    but Known │  Uncertainties
              │  ← THESE MATTER
   ───────────┼───────────
    Background│  Interesting
    Noise     │  but Marginal
              │
         Low Impact (영향력 낮음)
              │
    Low                    High
    Uncertainty            Uncertainty
    (확실)                  (불확실)
```

- **Top-right (Critical Uncertainties, 핵심 불확실성)**: These become your scenario axes
- **Top-left (Important but Known, 중요하지만 확실한 요인)**: These appear in ALL scenarios as "predetermined elements"
- **Bottom quadrants**: Set aside — they don't differentiate scenarios

### Step 4: Select 2 Critical Uncertainties → 2x2 Scenario Matrix (2x2 시나리오 매트릭스)

Choose the **two most critical uncertainties** — those with the highest combination of impact and uncertainty. These become the axes of your scenario matrix.

**Selection criteria**:
- Must be genuinely uncertain (not just unfavorable)
- Must be independent of each other (not correlated)
- Must create four meaningfully different futures when combined
- Must be relevant to the focal question

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                             │
│              CRITICAL UNCERTAINTY #1 →                      │
│              (e.g., Technology adoption speed)               │
│                                                             │
│         SLOW                              FAST              │
│          ┌──────────────────┬──────────────────┐            │
│          │                  │                  │            │
│     H    │   🌊 Scenario A  │   🚀 Scenario B  │            │
│     I    │                  │                  │            │
│     G    │  "Gradual Shift" │ "Green Sprint"   │            │
│     H    │                  │                  │            │
│  C       ├──────────────────┼──────────────────┤            │
│  R  L    │                  │                  │            │
│  I  O    │   🏔️ Scenario C  │   ⚡ Scenario D  │            │
│  T  W    │                  │                  │            │
│  I       │  "Status Quo     │ "Tech Islands"   │            │
│  C       │   Extended"      │                  │            │
│  A       │                  │                  │            │
│  L       └──────────────────┴──────────────────┘            │
│                                                             │
│  UNCERTAINTY #2 ↕                                           │
│  (e.g., Regulatory pressure)                                │
│                                                             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Each quadrant represents one plausible future — not a prediction, not a preference.**

### Step 5: Develop 4 Scenario Narratives (4개 시나리오 서사 개발)

Each scenario needs:

1. **A memorable name** (기억하기 쉬운 이름) — evocative, not judgmental (avoid "Best Case/Worst Case")
2. **A narrative arc** (서사 흐름) — how does the world get from here to there? What events unfold?
3. **Key characteristics** (주요 특성) — what does this world look, feel, and operate like?
4. **Implications for our focal question** (핵심 질문에 대한 시사점) — what happens to our industry, customers, competitors?
5. **Winners and losers** (승자와 패자) — who thrives and who struggles in this world?

**Narrative quality matters**. Scenarios must be:
- **Plausible** (그럴듯한) — not science fiction, could genuinely happen
- **Internally consistent** (내적 일관성) — the pieces fit together logically
- **Challenging** (도전적인) — each scenario should surprise or discomfort at least some stakeholders
- **Distinct** (구별되는) — clearly different from each other, not variations on a theme

### Step 6: Identify Signposts and Early Warning Signals (신호 및 조기경보 지표)

For each scenario, define **observable, measurable indicators** that would tell you that world is materializing:

| Signpost Type | Description | Example |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| **Leading indicators (선행지표)** | Early signals before the trend is obvious | Patent filings in industrial robotics |
| **Confirming indicators (확인지표)** | Validate that a scenario is unfolding | Regulatory announcements, competitor moves |
| **Disconfirming indicators (반증지표)** | Evidence that a scenario is NOT happening | Failed technology pilots, regulatory rollbacks |

**Build a monitoring dashboard** — assign responsibility for tracking each signpost on a regular cadence (monthly or quarterly).

### Step 7: Test Strategies Against All 4 Scenarios — Wind Tunneling (전략 풍동 테스트)

This is where scenario planning delivers its highest value. For each candidate strategy:

```
Strategy: "Invest heavily in automation capabilities"

              Scenario A    Scenario B    Scenario C    Scenario D
              (Gradual)     (Green Sprint) (Status Quo)  (Tech Islands)
  ┌─────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┬─────────────┐
  │ Revenue │    ↗ Modest  │  ↑↑ Strong  │   → Flat    │  ↗ Moderate │
  │ Risk    │     Medium   │     Low     │    High     │    Medium   │
  │ Fit     │   Adequate   │  Excellent  │    Poor     │    Good     │
  │ Verdict │      ✅       │     ✅✅      │      ❌      │      ✅      │
  └─────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┴─────────────┘
  Overall: Strong in 3/4 scenarios — CONDITIONALLY ROBUST
  Vulnerability: Scenario C — need hedge or contingency
```

**Scoring framework**:
- **Robust strategy (강건한 전략)**: Performs well across all or most scenarios
- **Conditional strategy (조건부 전략)**: Performs well in some, poorly in others — needs hedges
- **Fragile strategy (취약한 전략)**: Only works in one scenario — dangerous bet

### Step 8: Develop Robust Strategy + Contingency Triggers (강건한 전략 + 비상 트리거)

The final output combines:

1. **Core strategy (핵심 전략)**: Actions that make sense across ALL scenarios
2. **Scenario-specific options (시나리오별 옵션)**: Investments or capabilities to activate IF a specific scenario materializes
3. **Contingency triggers (비상 트리거)**: Pre-defined signpost thresholds that trigger strategic pivots
4. **No-regret moves (후회 없는 조치)**: Actions beneficial regardless of which future unfolds
5. **Big bets (대형 베팅)**: High-payoff actions for specific scenarios, taken only when signposts confirm

## Wind Tunneling in Detail (풍동 테스트 상세)

Wind tunneling is the practice of systematically testing a strategy, decision, or investment against each scenario. Named after the engineering practice of testing aircraft designs in wind tunnels before flight.

**Process**:

1. **Articulate the strategy clearly** — what exactly are we proposing to do?
2. **For each scenario, ask**:
   - Does this strategy still make sense in this world?
   - What revenue/profit impact would we expect?
   - What risks emerge that don't exist today?
   - What capabilities would we wish we had?
   - Would competitors respond differently?
3. **Score using a consistent rubric** (1-5 scale or traffic light)
4. **Identify vulnerabilities** — which scenarios break this strategy?
5. **Design hedges** — what low-cost actions could protect against the vulnerable scenarios?

**Wind tunneling questions (풍동 테스트 질문)**:
- "If we wake up in Scenario B, would we regret this investment?"
- "What's the earliest we'd know Scenario C is happening, and can we pivot fast enough?"
- "Is there a version of this strategy that works in all four scenarios?"

## Signpost Monitoring System (신호 모니터링 시스템)

Scenarios are not "set and forget." Build a living monitoring system:

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           SIGNPOST MONITORING DASHBOARD                │
├──────────────┬──────────┬───────────┬─────────────────┤
│   Signpost   │ Current  │ Scenario  │ Last Updated    │
│              │ Reading  │ Indicated │                 │
├──────────────┼──────────┼───────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Ind. robot   │ +15% YoY │ B or D   │ 2026-Q1         │
│ patent files │          │          │                 │
├──────────────┼──────────┼───────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Env. reg.    │ Moderate │ A or C   │ 2026-Q1         │
│ stringency   │ increase │          │                 │
├──────────────┼──────────┼───────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Labor cost   │ +8% YoY  │ B or D   │ 2026-Q1         │
│ growth rate  │          │          │                 │
├──────────────┼──────────┼───────────┼─────────────────┤
│ Client capex │ Flat     │ C        │ 2026-Q1         │
│ budgets      │          │          │                 │
└──────────────┴──────────┴───────────┴─────────────────┘

Convergence: Signals point toward Scenario B/D axis (technology-driven)

```

**Review cadence**: Quarterly at minimum, monthly during high-uncertainty periods.

## Common Pitfalls (흔한 실수)

### 1. Too Many Scenarios (시나리오 과다)
**Problem**: Building 6, 8, or more scenarios dilutes focus and overwhelms decision-makers.
**Fix**: Always use 2x2 = 4 scenarios. If you feel you need more, your axes aren't capturing the right uncertainties.

### 2. Assigning Probabilities (확률 배정)
**Problem**: Stakeholders want to know "which scenario is most likely?" This defeats the purpose — you'll just optimize for the "probable" one and ignore the others.
**Fix**: Refuse to assign probabilities. All scenarios are plausible. Strategy must work across all of them.

### 3. Good/Bad Scenarios (좋은/나쁜 시나리오)
**Problem**: Creating "best case" and "worst case" — these aren't scenarios, they're optimism and pessimism.
**Fix**: Each scenario should have opportunities AND threats. Name them evocatively, not evaluatively.

### 4. Predetermined Elements Masquerading as Uncertainties (확정요인의 불확실성 위장)
**Problem**: Including trends that are virtually certain (e.g., "population aging in Korea") as a scenario axis.
**Fix**: Predetermined elements go IN every scenario. Only genuine uncertainties become axes.

### 5. Scenarios as Forecasts (시나리오를 예측으로 오해)
**Problem**: After building scenarios, the team picks one as "our view" and plans accordingly.
**Fix**: Strategy must be tested against ALL scenarios. The whole point is avoiding single-future bets.

### 6. One-Time Exercise (일회성 작업)
**Problem**: Building scenarios once and never updating them.
**Fix**: Monitor signposts, refresh scenarios annually or when major disruptions occur.

### 7. Correlated Axes (상관된 축)
**Problem**: The two uncertainties move together (e.g., "economic growth" and "consumer spending" — these are not independent).
**Fix**: Test independence: can one axis be high while the other is low? If not, choose different axes.

## Sub-Commands (하위 명령)

### `/think:scenario-planning` — Full Process (전체 프로세스)

Runs the complete 8-step scenario planning process. Requires:
- A focal question or strategic decision
- Industry/company context
- Time horizon (default: 5 years)

Produces: Complete scenario planning report with all 8 steps.

### `/think:scenario-planning:drivers` — Identify Driving Forces (동인 분석)

Focused sub-command for Step 2-3 only. Use when:
- You need to brainstorm forces before committing to full analysis
- You want to validate your uncertainty/impact rankings
- You're updating an existing scenario set with new forces

Produces: PESTEL-organized driving force list with uncertainty/impact matrix.

### `/think:scenario-planning:test` — Wind-Tunnel a Strategy (전략 풍동 테스트)

Focused sub-command for Step 7 only. Use when:
- You already have scenarios defined
- You want to test a specific strategy, investment, or decision
- You're comparing multiple strategic options

Requires: Existing scenario definitions + strategy to test.
Produces: Wind tunnel scorecard with robustness assessment and hedge recommendations.
