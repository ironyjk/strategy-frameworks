# Strategy Map Guide — Building Cause-and-Effect Strategy

Reference for Kaplan & Norton's Strategy Maps (2004)

## What is a Strategy Map?

A strategy map is a one-page visual representation of an organization's strategy as a set of **cause-and-effect relationships** across the four BSC perspectives. It answers: "How does our strategy create value?"

The strategy map is the **missing link** between strategy formulation and the Balanced Scorecard. Without it, objectives in each perspective are disconnected metrics. With it, they tell a coherent story.

## Three Strategic Themes

Every strategy map should be built around one (or a blend) of three value disciplines:

### 1. Operational Excellence
"Best total cost" — lowest price, most convenient, zero defects

| Perspective | Key Objectives |
|-------------|---------------|
| Financial | Reduce cost per unit, improve asset utilization |
| Customer | Competitive pricing, consistent quality, fast delivery |
| Internal | Streamlined operations, supply chain efficiency, quality management |
| Learning | Process improvement skills, lean/six sigma, ERP systems |

**Example chain**: Lean training (L&G) -> Reduce cycle time (Process) -> Faster delivery (Customer) -> Lower cost, higher margin (Financial)

### 2. Customer Intimacy
"Best total solution" — deep relationships, customized solutions

| Perspective | Key Objectives |
|-------------|---------------|
| Financial | Customer lifetime value growth, share of wallet |
| Customer | Customized solutions, trusted advisor relationship |
| Internal | Customer knowledge management, solution development, relationship management |
| Learning | Customer analytics, CRM systems, consultative selling skills |

**Example chain**: CRM system + training (L&G) -> Deep customer knowledge (Process) -> Tailored solutions (Customer) -> Revenue growth per customer (Financial)

### 3. Product Leadership
"Best product" — cutting-edge, first to market, premium performance

| Perspective | Key Objectives |
|-------------|---------------|
| Financial | Revenue from new products, premium pricing |
| Customer | Superior functionality, first-to-market features |
| Internal | R&D pipeline, rapid prototyping, speed to market |
| Learning | Creative talent, collaboration tools, innovation culture |

**Example chain**: Innovation culture + talent (L&G) -> Rapid R&D (Process) -> Best-in-class product (Customer) -> New product revenue (Financial)

## Building a Strategy Map: Step by Step

### Step 1: Start with Financial Objectives
What do shareholders/owners expect?
- Revenue growth (top line)
- Productivity/cost improvement (bottom line)
- Asset utilization (efficiency)

Typical pattern: balance **revenue growth** with **productivity improvement**.

### Step 2: Define the Customer Value Proposition
Who is the target customer? What will they value most?

Use the value proposition template:
```
For [target customer segment]
Who [has this need/problem]
Our [product/service]
Provides [these differentiating benefits]
Unlike [the alternative/competitor]
```

### Step 3: Identify Critical Internal Processes
Which processes must we excel at to deliver the value proposition?

Four process clusters:
1. **Operations Management**: supply chain, production, delivery, risk management
2. **Customer Management**: selection, acquisition, retention, growth
3. **Innovation**: identify opportunities, R&D portfolio, design/develop, launch
4. **Regulatory & Social**: environment, safety, employment, community

Typically 2-3 critical processes drive 80% of the strategy.

### Step 4: Define Learning & Growth Foundation
What intangible assets must be in place?

Three categories:
1. **Human Capital**: skills, training, knowledge workers
2. **Information Capital**: databases, systems, networks, technology infrastructure
3. **Organization Capital**: culture, leadership, alignment, teamwork

### Step 5: Draw the Cause-and-Effect Arrows
Connect objectives across perspectives. Every objective should have at least one arrow pointing to and from it. If an objective has no arrows, it's either isolated (remove it) or missing links (add them).

### Step 6: Validate the Story
Read the map as a narrative: "IF we invest in [L&G], THEN [process] will improve, which means [customer] will experience [value], resulting in [financial outcome]."

If the story doesn't hold up logically, revise.

## Strategy Map Template (Blank)

```
FINANCIAL:
  [ Objective 1 ]    [ Objective 2 ]    [ Objective 3 ]
        |                  |                  |
CUSTOMER:
  [ Objective 4 ]    [ Objective 5 ]    [ Objective 6 ]
        |                  |                  |
INTERNAL PROCESS:
  [ Objective 7 ]    [ Objective 8 ]    [ Objective 9 ]
        |                  |                  |
LEARNING & GROWTH:
  [ Objective 10 ]   [ Objective 11 ]   [ Objective 12 ]
```

Guidelines:
- 12-20 objectives total (3-5 per perspective)
- Every objective linked by cause-and-effect arrows
- One clear value proposition theme
- Financial objectives split between growth and productivity

---

## Common Mistakes in Strategy Maps

1. **No cause-and-effect links** — just four lists of objectives without connections
2. **Too many objectives** — trying to be everything to everyone. Focus.
3. **Mixing strategy with operations** — "answer phones within 3 rings" is operational, not strategic
4. **Missing Learning & Growth** — this is the foundation; skipping it means no investment in future capability
5. **Financial-only** — if only financial objectives have arrows pointing to them, the map is unbalanced
6. **No value proposition clarity** — trying to be lowest cost AND most innovative AND most intimate simultaneously
7. **Static map** — strategy maps should evolve as the strategy hypothesis is tested and refined

```
FINANCIAL:
  [Revenue growth     [Improve project    [Reduce equipment
   from key accounts]  margins to 15%]     idle time cost]
        |                    |                   |
CUSTOMER:
  [Trusted safety     [Rapid response     [Competitive
   & compliance        within 24hrs]       pricing through
   partner]                                 efficiency]
        |                    |                   |
INTERNAL PROCESS:
  [Zero-incident      [Streamlined         [Equipment
   safety record]      dispatch &           utilization
                        scheduling]          optimization]
        |                    |                   |
LEARNING & GROWTH:
  [Safety training    [Digital dispatch    [Preventive
   & certification]    system]              maintenance
                                            skills]
```

Narrative: By investing in safety training, digital dispatch, and maintenance skills, we achieve zero incidents, fast response, and high equipment utilization. This makes us the trusted, fast, cost-competitive partner — driving revenue growth, better margins, and lower costs.
