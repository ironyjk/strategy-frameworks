# KPI Examples by Industry

Balanced Scorecard KPIs organized by perspective and industry.

## Universal KPIs (Applicable Across Industries)

### Financial Perspective
| KPI | Formula | Typical Target |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Revenue Growth Rate | (Revenue_current - Revenue_prior) / Revenue_prior | 5-20% YoY |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue | Industry-dependent |
| Return on Assets (ROA) | Net Income / Total Assets | 5-15% |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | Net Income / Shareholders' Equity | 10-20% |
| Cash Conversion Cycle | DSO + DIO - DPO | Lower is better |
| Revenue per Employee | Total Revenue / FTE | Benchmark vs. peers |
| Economic Value Added (EVA) | NOPAT - (Capital x WACC) | Positive |

### Customer Perspective
| KPI | Formula | Typical Target |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Customer Satisfaction (CSAT) | Survey score | 4.0+/5.0 |
| Net Promoter Score (NPS) | %Promoters - %Detractors | 30+ (good), 50+ (excellent) |
| Customer Retention Rate | (Customers_end - New) / Customers_start | 85-95% |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | Marketing+Sales spend / New customers | Lower is better |
| Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) | Avg revenue x Avg lifespan x Margin | CLV > 3x CAC |
| Share of Wallet | Our revenue / Customer's total spend in category | Increasing |
| On-Time Delivery | Orders delivered on time / Total orders | 95%+ |

### Internal Process Perspective
| KPI | Formula | Typical Target |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Process Cycle Time | Total time from start to finish | Decreasing |
| First Pass Yield | Units without rework / Total units | 95%+ |
| Capacity Utilization | Actual output / Maximum output | 75-85% |
| Defect Rate | Defects / Total units | <1% |
| Order Fulfillment Cycle | Time from order to delivery | Industry benchmark |
| Innovation Pipeline | # projects in each stage | Healthy funnel |
| Incident Rate | Safety incidents / Hours worked | Zero target |

### Learning & Growth Perspective
| KPI | Formula | Typical Target |
|-----|---------|---------------|
| Employee Engagement Score | Survey score | 4.0+/5.0 |
| Training Hours per Employee | Total training hours / FTE | 40+ hours/year |
| Employee Turnover Rate | Departures / Avg headcount | <10-15% |
| Internal Promotion Rate | Internal fills / Total positions filled | 60%+ |
| Skills Coverage | Employees with required certifications / Total | 90%+ |
| System Availability | Uptime / Total time | 99.5%+ |
| Knowledge Sharing | Contributions to knowledge base | Increasing |

## KPI Design Checklist

When selecting KPIs for any BSC, verify each one against:

- [ ] **Aligned**: Directly linked to a strategic objective on the strategy map
- [ ] **Measurable**: Can be quantified with available data (or data we can reasonably collect)
- [ ] **Actionable**: The team can influence this metric through their actions
- [ ] **Timely**: Data available frequently enough to drive decisions (monthly minimum)
- [ ] **Balanced**: Mix of leading indicators (predictive) and lagging indicators (outcome)
- [ ] **Owned**: One person is accountable for each KPI
- [ ] **Benchmarked**: We know what "good" looks like (internal trend or external benchmark)
- [ ] **Not gamed**: Difficult to improve the number without improving the reality

### Leading vs. Lagging Indicators

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Lagging** | Measures outcomes (after the fact) | Revenue, profit, customer satisfaction |
| **Leading** | Measures drivers (predictive) | Training hours, pipeline value, proposal win rate |

A good BSC has **both**: lagging indicators tell you if you succeeded; leading indicators tell you if you're on track to succeed.
